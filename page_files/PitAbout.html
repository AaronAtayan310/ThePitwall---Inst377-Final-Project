About the project

The
Pitwall

Formula 1, or F1 for short, is the pinnacle of motorsport. With over 20 races in 21 countries spread across five continents in just one single year, it can be difficult to keep track of the statistics, news, and on-track drama of the fastest people in the world, especially for casual fans of the sport and those who simply are unable to tune in to race weekends. To solve this problem, I have created The Pitwall.

The Pitwall provides fans with live and up to date information and news all about F1. The sites name references the pitwall, which is the trackside command center where engineers and strategists of each team monitor every sensor on the car and make real-time calls that decide the outcome of a race. That same data is now on your browser!

ThePitwall was created for INST377: Dynamic Web Applications at the University of Maryland. It uses the OpenF1 API to pull live and historical session data, caches it in Supabase to stay within rate limits, and serves it through a JavaScript frontend and a Node/Express backend deployed on Vercel.

Built by Aaron Atayan — Spring 2026

What you can do
01
Race Schedule
Browse the full season calendar—every Grand Prix weekend with session breakdowns, circuits, and local times.
02
Driver Profiles
See every driver on the grid: number, team, nationality, and headshot pulled directly from OpenF1.
03
Live Lap Times
Live leaderboard with per-driver sector times, current tyre compound, and time gaps to the leader.
04
Car Telemetry
Real-time gear selection, DRS status, brake traces, and speed data for any driver in any session.
05
Pit Strategy
Track all pit stops—lap numbers, stop durations, tyre compounds—for every car throughout the race.
